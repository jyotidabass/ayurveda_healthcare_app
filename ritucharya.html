{% blocktrans %} Recommendation for Kaphaj prakṛti - śiśir ṛtu (mid-January to mid-March) {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}
  • Cereals – barley, corn, millet, rye, buckwheat. Cereals in limited quantiy - oat, rice, wheat.
  • Pulses – Moog daal, red lentil. Pulses in limited quantity - kidney beans, chickpeas, pinto, Lima, Soy and split peas.
  • Oils – flaxseed, sunflower oil, ghee.
  • Milk products – low fat milk, yogurt or ghee made of low fat milk, goat’s milk, sheep’s milk, soya milk. Milk should be taken along with teaspoon of turmeric or ginger which will reduce kapha increasing quality of milk. Dairy products are known to increase kapha so better avoid them or consume low fat, calorie version.
  • Vegetables – sweet bitter veggies like asparagus, cabbage, carrot, garlic, radishes, onion, bean sprouts, green beans, peppers, mushrooms, beetroot, etc.
  • Fruits – apple, pear, watermelon, pomegranate, apricot, cranberries. To be consumed in less quantity: bananas, pineapple, oranges, coconut, dates.
  • Drinks - herbal tea, teas with cinnamon, ginger and bitter substances, soups are best pacifying kapha.
  • Meat - chiken, turkey, eggs, prawns.
  • Seed and dry fruits - almond, chia seeds, pumpkin seed, sunflower seeds.
  • Sweeteners - except for honey other sweeteners are better avoided.
  • Exercise – Kapha dominant people have highest endurance of all the doshas and can perform any exercise for long period of time. It's best that kapha person to exercise in the kapha time (early morning) of day.
  • Ritucharya – kapha increases in shirish ritu so kapha person avoid eating too sweet, fatty, heavy meals during this season. Engage in moderate exercise and follow shishir ritu charya.
{% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %} Recommendation for Pittaj prakṛti – śarad ṛtu (mid-September to mid-November) {% endblocktrans %}

{% blocktrans %}
  • Cereals – whole wheat, Oats, barley, white rice. In limited quantity - white refined flour, Rye, White Rice, Dry oats.
  • Pulses – Moog daal, red lentil. In limited quantity - kidney beans, chickpeas, pinto, Lima, Soy and split peas.
  • Oils - cow ghee, olive oil, coconut oil, sunflower oil.
  • Milk products – Buttermilk, cow milk, unsalted butter. In limited quantity - ice cream, powdered milk, whole milk, full fat yogurt, cream cheese, sour cream.
  • Vegetables – sweet bitter veggies like asparagus, cabbage, cucumber, cauliflower, cooked onion, Beets, carrots, cilantro, green beans, potatoes, mushrooms, etc. In limited quantity: Radish, garlic, tomatoes, onion.
  • Fruits – sweet fruits like Avocado, figs, plums, orange, pomegranates, melon, grapes, mangos, bananas. Should not eat fruits in evening or before or after meals, also sour fruits like apple, lemon, dried fruits avoided.
  • Drinks - plenty of cooling water (body temperature), mellow, sweet juices, fennel tea.
  • Meat - meat to be consumed in less quantity in this season as it may aggravate pitta dosha. Eggs in moderate, other meat and fish products should be avoided.
  • Seed and dry fruits - stick with cooling nuts almond, sunflower seed (all to be taken soaked).
  • Sweeteners - Most natural sugars in small amounts, try to avoid old honey molassess and white sugar.
  • Exercise – pitta prakruti person will get benefit from cooling, slow, dense activities like moderate hiking, swimming , weight lifting, yoga.
  • Pitta dosha increases in śarad ritu so the detoxication process Virecanā is advised in this season.
{% endblocktrans %}
